What is the IDB Accelerator Program, and who is it for?

The IDB Accelerator Program (Ideate, Design & Build) is an initiative by Open Source Community Africa designed to empower individuals from all backgrounds to contribute to open-source projects. Our program provides hands-on experience, mentorship, and resources to help both technical and non-technical participants develop innovative, real-world solutions. If you're passionate about open-source, eager to learn, and interested in creating meaningful impact—this program is for you.

Do I need a technical background to join the IDB Accelerator?

No, you don’t need to be a developer or engineer to participate! We welcome both technical and non-technical talents, including designers, project managers, quality assurance testers, and anyone eager to contribute to open-source. Our program offers mentorship across various roles to ensure everyone has a place and opportunity to make an impact.

What kind of projects will I work on in the program?

Participants will work on a variety of open-source projects, ranging from software development to design and project management. You may either join an existing project or propose a new idea to explore with the support of our mentors. Projects are designed to solve real-world problems, giving you experience that’s both relevant and impactful.

Can companies or organizations partner with the IDB Accelerator?

Absolutely! We’re always looking to partner with organizations that believe in open-source innovation and are interested in supporting diverse talents. Partnering with us provides organizations with visibility within the open-source community, access to emerging tech talent, and opportunities for collaborative projects. If your organization would like to explore partnership options, please reach out to us!

How can sponsors support the IDB Accelerator, and what benefits do they receive?

Sponsors play a crucial role in the success of the IDB Accelerator. Through sponsorship, your organization can support participant resources, mentorship, and community events. In return, sponsors gain visibility within the tech community, branding opportunities, and access to a network of talented open-source contributors. If you’re interested in sponsoring, contact us to learn more about how we can work together to drive impact.

Is there a selection process for participants?

Yes, we have a selection process to ensure that each cohort is aligned with the program’s goals and ready to make the most of the experience. Applicants complete an application form, submit a resume, and may undergo a technical review and interview. We’re looking for individuals who show a strong interest in open-source, creativity, and collaboration.

What happens after the program? Are there ongoing opportunities for alumni?

Upon completing the program, participants join our growing alumni network, which offers continued mentorship, networking, and project opportunities. Alumni also receive support for securing funding or launching their open-source projects into startups. We aim to create long-term impact by helping alumni stay engaged in open-source and further their professional growth.

What resources and support are provided to participants?

The IDB Accelerator offers participants access to skilled mentors, industry connections, and various tools needed for project development. You’ll have the support and guidance to navigate each stage, from ideation to execution, ensuring that your experience is both enriching and productive.
